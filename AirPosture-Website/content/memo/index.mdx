# What We Learned

Current retention declined steadily over time. User interviews revealed a clear gap: 

Posture awareness alone isn’t enough — users want active relief for neck, back, and shoulder pain.  

This insight directly drives our next phase: 

Guided stretching and relief-focused movement, not just posture awareness.

# Where We Are Going: Sensors + AI


### AirPods Will Be Bigger Than Apple Watch in Health

AirPods are growing fast — with 18.2 million units shipped in Q1 2025, up 12% year-over-year, while Apple Watch shipments declined ~19% in 2024.

### Richer Sensors + On-Device AI

At the same time, AirPods now include **richer motion and medical-grade heart rate sensors**, and Apple’s MLX framework **enables private, low-latency AI models on iOS** without cloud delays or privacy compromises.

### IR Camera (2026)

![content overview](/media/ir2.jpeg)

Looking ahead, top sources [(Bloomberg)](https://x.com/markgurman/status/1960011255361454170), [郭明錤 (Ming-Chi Kuo)](https://x.com/mingchikuo/status/1964533958722670804), [9to5mac](https://9to5mac.com/2025/10/31/next-years-airpods-lineup-could-be-apples-biggest-ever/) all suggest next-gen AirPods may include infrared (IR) cameras in 2026. This could unlock an entirely new posture ecosystem, using **IR scanning through the ear** to capture precise body alignment in real time.

This combination of **rapidly expanding user base**, **advanced health sensors**, and **emerging IR technology** makes today the perfect moment for AirPosture.
